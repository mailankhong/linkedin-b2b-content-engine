# Templates Library

20 templates across 5 categories. Each template: name, use conditions, hook formulas (3 max), body structure, CTA options.

Referenced by: copy-developer (ENTRY ROUTING), hook-generator (Phase 2)

---

## TEACH / EDUCATIONAL

Goal: demonstrate expertise and give immediate value. Tier 2 and Tier 3 ICP. Drives saves and follows.

---

### T1 — The Step-by-Step How-To

**Use when:** You have a repeatable process that delivers a specific outcome and can be broken into discrete steps. The process must be counterintuitive or unknown to your audience — a known process is not worth posting.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "The [X]-step system I use to [outcome]. Step by step:"
2. "Here's exactly how I [specific process] — including the part everyone skips:"
3. "Most [audience] [approach thing wrong]. Here's the right way:"

**Body structure:** Name the problem or gap → introduce the framework or process name → walk through steps in order (numbered) → close with the insight that makes it work.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "Which step are you on right now?"
- Promotional: "DM me [word] and I'll send you the full template."

---

### T2 — The Common Mistake Fix

**Use when:** You have observed the same mistake repeatedly across clients, calls, or your own experience. The mistake must be specific — a named behavior, not a category of behavior. "Bad follow-up timing" is specific. "Poor communication" is not.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "Most [audience] [do specific thing wrong]. Here's what actually works:"
2. "[Specific mistake] is costing [audience] [specific outcome]. Here's why:"
3. "Stop [common action]. It's killing your [important metric]."

**Body structure:** Name the mistake precisely → explain why people do it (the logic that makes it feel right) → show what happens as a result → present the correct approach → one-line takeaway.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What mistake have you seen show up most often in [area]?"
- Promotional: "If you're making this one, there are usually 2 more. DM me to find them."

---

### T3 — The Tool Recommendation

**Use when:** You have used a specific tool long enough to have an honest opinion, including trade-offs. Honest assessments outperform endorsements. Include the context where it does and doesn't work — posts without trade-offs read as ads.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Tool] replaced [old method / more expensive thing] for me. Here's how:"
2. "I've tested [N] tools for [task]. The one I still use after [timeframe]:"
3. "Honest review of [tool] after [timeframe] of use:"

**Body structure:** Context (what you needed to solve) → what you tried → what you landed on → what it does well → where it falls short → recommendation with the right use case.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What's your current setup for [task]?"
- Promotional: "Helping [audience] build the right stack is part of what I do. DM me if you want a quick look at yours."

---

### T4 — The Behind-the-Scenes Process

**Use when:** You can show how something actually gets done — not the polished result, but the working method. The process must be something your ICP is curious about but cannot see elsewhere.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "Here's exactly how I [specific process] — nothing cut:"
2. "What [deliverable / outcome] actually looks like before it's finished:"
3. "[N] years ago I would have hidden this. Here's my real [process]:"

**Body structure:** What the thing is and why it matters → walk through how it actually happens (specific, with friction included) → what you would do differently now → the principle others can take from it.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What part of your process do you wish more people understood?"
- Promotional: "This is how I work with clients on [outcome]. If that's what you need, let's talk."

## LEAD / THOUGHT LEADERSHIP

Goal: claim a distinct POV and build credibility. Tier 1 and Tier 2 ICP. Drives follows and connection requests.

---

### L1 — The Contrarian Take

**Use when:** You hold a belief that runs counter to conventional wisdom in your industry, and you have experience or evidence to support it. Do not manufacture controversy — the belief must be genuinely yours and genuinely supported.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Common belief in the industry] is wrong. Here's what's actually happening:"
2. "Everyone says [X]. I disagree. Here's why:"
3. "[Widely accepted practice] is making [audience] worse at [outcome]."

**Body structure:** State the common belief → acknowledge why it feels true → present the counter-evidence or counter-experience → state your actual position clearly → one-line implication for the reader.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "Where do you land on this? I'm genuinely curious who disagrees."
- Promotional: avoid — this post type earns trust, not leads.

---

### L2 — The Pattern Recognition

**Use when:** You have observed the same pattern across multiple clients, conversations, or situations and can name it. The pattern must be specific enough that a reader recognizes it immediately — "clients who do X tend to see Y" is specific. "Success patterns" is not.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "I've [worked with / talked to / seen] [N] [audience type]. The pattern is always the same:"
2. "After [X years / N clients], I finally named what I kept seeing. Here's the pattern:"
3. "[N] conversations this [month/quarter]. Same thing came up every time:"

**Body structure:** State the pattern in one sentence → show it through 2–3 specific examples or data points → explain why the pattern exists → state what it means for the reader.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "Have you seen this in your work?"
- Promotional: avoid — save the ask for the next post.

---

### L3 — The Market Observation

**Use when:** Something is shifting in your industry — a behavior, a buyer dynamic, a technology trend — and you have a perspective on what it means that isn't obvious. Must be published close to the observation. A 3-month-old observation is not a market signal.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Something in the industry] is changing. Most [audience] haven't noticed yet."
2. "[Event / data point / shift] just happened. Here's what it actually means:"
3. "The [industry conversation] nobody's having yet:"

**Body structure:** State what you observed → show the evidence (specific, recent) → explain the implication that isn't obvious → state what the smart move is.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What are you seeing on your end?"
- Promotional: avoid — authority posts build pipeline passively.

---

### L4 — The Framework Breakdown

**Use when:** You have developed or refined a mental model, framework, or system that helps explain something complex. The framework must be named — named frameworks travel further than unnamed observations.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "After [experience], I built a framework for [problem]. I call it [name]:"
2. "[Common problem]. Here's the framework I use to solve it:"
3. "There are [N] types of [thing]. Here's how to tell them apart:"

**Body structure:** Name the problem the framework solves → introduce the framework name → walk through the components (2–4 is ideal) → show how the parts connect → practical application in one sentence.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "Where does this break down for you?"
- Promotional: "I use this framework with every client I work with. If you want to apply it to your [situation], DM me."

## PROVE / SOCIAL PROOF

Goal: demonstrate specific results and attract similar buyers. Tier 1 and Tier 2 ICP. Drives DMs and direct pipeline.

---

### P1 — The Client Success Story

**Use when:** A client achieved a specific, measurable result you helped produce. The result must be named with a number. The more specific the before and after, the more persuasive the post.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Client description] went from [before state] to [after state] in [timeframe]."
2. "[Specific metric] in [timeframe]. Here's the story:"
3. "[Client's situation] was [problem]. Here's how that changed:"

**Body structure:** Who the client is (role, context — not name unless approved) → what the situation was before → what the specific challenge or blocker was → what you did together → the result with a number → the principle that made it work.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "If you're in [similar situation], what's the thing standing in your way right now?"
- Promotional: "If this sounds like where you are, let's talk about what it would take."

---

### P2 — The Results Announcement

**Use when:** You have a metric worth sharing — a business result, a client outcome, a personal milestone. Give context on what produced the result, not just the number. A number without context is a brag. A number with context is a lesson.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Specific metric] in [timeframe]. Here's what we did:"
2. "The result: [outcome]. Here's what actually happened:"
3. "[Before/after numbers]. What changed:"

**Body structure:** Lead with the number → provide the context (what it was before, what the goal was) → explain the 2–3 key decisions or actions that drove it → the transferable lesson.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What metric are you most focused on right now?"
- Promotional: "This is the kind of work I do with [ICP type]. If that's you, DM me."

---

### P3 — The Case Study Snapshot

**Use when:** You can show the full arc: specific situation, specific intervention, specific result. The most concrete elements → the most persuasive post. This is the highest-conversion post type for B2B pipeline.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Before state]. [After state]. Here's the exact intervention:"
2. "[Client description] hired me to [task]. Here's what we found, what we did, and what happened:"
3. "What [N months] of [work type] actually looks like — a real example:"

**Body structure:** Before (specific situation, specific problem) → the intervention (what was diagnosed, what was changed) → the result (specific metric, with timeframe) → the insight that would apply to a reader in the same situation.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What's the thing you're trying to move right now?"
- Promotional: "I work with [ICP type] on exactly this. If this is you, let's have a conversation."

---

### P4 — The Social Proof Stack

**Use when:** You have multiple data points — results across clients, repeated outcomes, a pattern of wins — that are more powerful combined than individually. Works best when the pattern speaks to a specific ICP.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[N] clients. [Same challenge]. [Consistent result]. The common thread:"
2. "Over the past [timeframe], here's what I've seen work — across [N] different situations:"
3. "[N] [results type] in [timeframe]. What they all had in common:"

**Body structure:** State the pattern claim → give 3–4 specific data points (each in 1–2 lines) → name the common thread → state the implication for the reader.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What's the version of this in your work?"
- Promotional: "All [N] of these happened inside a [program/service description]. If you want that result, let's talk."

## CONNECT / RELATIONSHIP

Goal: build trust through vulnerability and shared experience. All tiers — humanizes the expert. Drives follows and long-term loyalty.

**Rule:** Connect posts do not carry promotional CTAs. The trust they build is the conversion. Do not attach a sales ask.

---

### C1 — The Personal Story

**Use when:** You have a real experience — professional or personal — that shaped how you think about your work or your life. The story must have a turning point. Observations without a turning point are not stories — they are reflections. Use C3 instead.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Year]. I [thing that happened]. It changed everything I thought I knew about [topic]."
2. "[Specific moment]. I didn't know it then, but everything was about to change."
3. "The [professional moment] I almost didn't share:"

**Body structure:** Set the scene (specific time, place, or situation) → what happened → the turning point or moment of change → what you took from it → what this means for the reader (briefly, without preaching).

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "If this hit home, I'd love to know why."
- No promotional CTA.

---

### C2 — The Mistake and Lesson

**Use when:** You made a specific, identifiable mistake in your professional life and can name what you learned from it. The mistake must be real — not a humblebrag. The lesson must be specific — "I learned to be patient" is not a lesson.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "I used to [thing they do too]. Then [event] happened. Here's what I learned:"
2. "[Specific mistake] cost me [specific outcome]. Here's the honest version of what happened:"
3. "[Timeframe] ago, I made a call I regret. Here's what I'd do now:"

**Body structure:** Name the mistake specifically → explain the reasoning that made it feel right at the time → what happened as a result → what you understand now that you didn't then → the lesson stated plainly.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What's the professional mistake you learned the most from?"
- No promotional CTA.

---

### C3 — The Question Starter

**Use when:** You want to surface the community's perspective on a real question — one you genuinely don't know the answer to, or where the range of answers will be valuable. Do not ask questions you've already answered or that are designed to make you look smart.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "A question I keep coming back to: [question]"
2. "I've asked [N] people this question and gotten [N] different answers. Here it is:"
3. "[Context that makes the question real.] So here's what I want to know:"

**Body structure:** Give enough context for the question to land (why this is on your mind, why it matters now) → ask the question plainly → optionally share your current thinking without closing it off → invite honest responses.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "I'll respond to every answer. What do you think?"
- No promotional CTA.

---

### C4 — The Journey Post

**Use when:** You are marking a genuine milestone, transition, or moment of progress in your professional journey. Share what the milestone cost, not just what it achieved — cost is what makes it real.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[N] years ago I [starting point]. Here's what nobody told me:"
2. "[Milestone] just happened. Here's what it actually took:"
3. "I hit [marker]. What I know now that I didn't know at the start:"

**Body structure:** State the milestone or transition → what the starting point looked like → the 2–3 things that were harder than expected → what you'd tell someone starting where you started → close with gratitude or forward momentum (brief, not saccharine).

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What's the thing you wish someone had told you earlier in your journey?"
- No promotional CTA.

## RESPOND / AMPLIFY

Goal: claim relevance by responding to real events in real time. All tiers — builds authority through timeliness. Drives follows and shares.

**Rule:** Respond posts rarely carry a hard CTA. The value is in the perspective, not the ask. Publish within 24–48 hours of the triggering event.

---

### R1 — The Industry Response

**Use when:** A news event, product announcement, regulatory change, or public debate has surfaced in your industry and you have a genuine opinion on what it means for your ICP. Do not summarize the event — respond to it. If you can't add perspective, don't post.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Event] just happened. Here's what it actually means for [audience]:"
2. "The [industry announcement] nobody's talking about clearly enough:"
3. "[Event]. My take — and why it matters more than it looks:"

**Body structure:** State what happened (briefly — one sentence, assume they've seen it) → your immediate reaction → the non-obvious implication → what the smart response is → close with the one thing you'd be watching.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What's your read on this?"
- No promotional CTA.

---

### R2 — The Event Recap

**Use when:** You have attended a conference, workshop, or meaningful professional gathering and want to share what you took away. One strong insight outperforms a list of observations. Publish within 24–48 hours.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[N] days at [event]. The one thing that stuck:"
2. "Just left [event]. Here's what I'm thinking about:"
3. "[Event] recap — but only the part worth keeping:"

**Body structure:** Where you were and why (1 sentence) → the moment or conversation that mattered most → what it surfaced or shifted for you → how you'll apply it → brief invitation for others who were there or have context.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What was your biggest takeaway from [event], if you were there?"
- No promotional CTA.

---

### R3 — The Milestone Update

**Use when:** You have hit a number, reached a mark, or crossed a threshold worth acknowledging. Keep it brief. The context matters more than the number. Do not let milestone posts become humble-brags.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "[Specific milestone]. What it actually took:"
2. "[Number] [thing]. Here's the honest version:"
3. "[Marker crossed]. Not what I expected."

**Body structure:** State the milestone (one line) → what the starting point looked like → 2 things that were harder than expected, 1 thing that was easier → the person or decision that mattered most → close with what's next (briefly).

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What marker are you working toward right now?"
- No promotional CTA.

---

### R4 — The Fresh Learning Drop

**Use when:** You just learned something — read something, had a conversation, made a connection — and the insight is worth sharing before it gets processed into a polished post. Publish within 48 hours. Raw and direct beats polished and generic here.

**Hook formulas:**
1. "Just [read / heard / realized] something. Here's the unprocessed version:"
2. "[Insight]. I'm still sitting with this."
3. "[Context — where you were, what you were doing]. The thing I can't stop thinking about:"

**Body structure:** What you encountered → why it hit differently than expected → the specific thing it changed or challenged → one implication you're sitting with → leave it open.

**CTA options:**
- Engagement: "What's the last thing you read or heard that genuinely changed how you think?"
- No promotional CTA.

## Weekly Content Calendar

Use the category mix as a guide, not a rule. Adjust based on personal material available.

### 3 posts per week
- 1 Teach
- 1 Lead or Prove
- 1 Connect

### 4 posts per week
- 1 Teach
- 1 Lead
- 1 Prove
- 1 Connect

### 5 posts per week
- 2 Teach
- 1 Lead
- 1 Prove
- 1 Connect
- (optional: swap one Teach for a Respond if a timely event warrants it)

**Category roles:**
- Teach and Lead → build the audience
- Prove → converts them
- Connect → retains them
- Respond → shows you're alive in the conversation

## Verification

**Correctness:**
```
□ Template selected matches the category identified in copy-developer STEP 1? Yes → pass. No → re-select.
□ Body structure in the draft follows the template's specified sequence? Yes → pass. No → reorder the draft sections.
□ CTA type matches the category rule? (Connect = no promotional CTA. Lead = no promotional CTA in most cases.) Yes → pass. No → fix the CTA.
```

**Completeness:**
```
□ Template selection states: category, template name, and body structure — before writing begins? Yes → pass. No → state them.
□ Hook selected from the category-specific formulas in Phase 2 of hook-generator? Yes → pass. No → run hook-generator with the correct category.
□ All required body sections present (as listed in the template)? Yes → pass. No → add missing sections.
```

**Context-fit:**
```
□ Template is appropriate for the client's ICP tier? (Prove posts are Tier 1–2. Teach posts work for Tier 2–3.) Yes → pass. No → reconsider the template.
□ The "use when" condition is met? (e.g., T2 requires a specific named mistake — not a category of behavior.) Yes → pass. No → switch to a more appropriate template.
□ Template is not overused — not the same template as the last 2 posts for this client? Yes → pass. No → select a different template.
```

If the draft follows this template as written and is published: what structural element is most likely to be missing or weak?
